**1. Why Visit Cambodia?**

We haven’t explored much of Asia before, but Cambodia changed that. With careful timing (May), we managed to avoid overwhelming crowds and experience the magical temples of Angkor Wat in peace.

From heart-wrenching history in Phnom Penh to serene sunrises at Angkor Wat, this **Cambodia travel** experience was unlike any other.

### **🏛️ Phnom Penh – Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum**

A moving visit to the **S21 Prison**, where we met survivor **Mr. Bou Meng**, one of only 7 to live through the horrors of Khmer Rouge rule.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-19.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-21.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-23.jpg)

Around mid day to we visited the **Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum**, a tragic reminder of the genocide of the communist Khmer Rouge led by Pol Pot in the 1970s. We sought to see and understand this senseless reign of terror and at the end there was the meeting with one of only 7 survivors of the genoice, Mr. Bou Meng. In his book, he talks about how he was miraculously spared because of his paintbrush. Even though he could not save his wife or children, he was determined to bear witness and stand in a courtroom to tell the world of the horrors that unfolded behind these prison cells. Around 2 million Cambodians were killed between 1975 and 1979 by the Khmer Rouge. In this high school converted to a prison, around 18,000 were tortured and killed. Only 7 survived and Mr. Bou Meng was one of them. A story of courage, survival and truth. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-25.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-27.jpg)

**🌳 Ta Prohm – The Jungle Temple**

Made famous by **Tomb Raider**, this temple is slowly being reclaimed by nature. Ancient stones wrapped in giant tree roots — simply breathtaking.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-58.jpg)

I am not big on architecture or history. This place won me over simply because it was a place that was losing it’s battle to nature. Once you start making your way through the maze of the temple complex, you will realize that the entire temple is being swallowed by the jungle. I believe this is how the world was meant to be. One with the natural world around in every way.

### **😊 Bayon Temple – The Smiling Faces**

Known as the **Face Temple**, Bayon Temple has **216 smiling Buddha faces** carved into 54 towers.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-73.jpg)

Known as the 'face temple' thanks to its iconic structures, huge heads glared down at us, exuding power and a hint of all-knowing kindness in the smile! 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-74.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-76.jpg)

**🏞️ Phnom Bok – Hidden Gem**

Off the beaten track, this mountaintop temple is **quiet, scenic, and worth the 600-step climb**. Ideal for sunset and reflection.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-78.jpg)

This place is truly a hidden gem that sits on top of a hill that overlooks all of Siem Reap. There is a sense of mystery surrounding these ruins, that was once a flourishing temple. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-79.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-81.jpg)

A visit to Phnom Bok is the best way to experience the Khmer region in all its (former) glory. You will get a sense of what once was. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/cambodia-travel-angkor-wat-itinerary-guide-83.jpg)
