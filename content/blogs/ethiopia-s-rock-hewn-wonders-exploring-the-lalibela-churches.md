**Flights & Visa for Ethiopia**

I flew in from Djibouti via **Ethiopian Airlines**, arriving late at night. The **Ethiopian e-visa** was seamless—30-day single-entry visa cost $80, processed online via the[ ](https://www.evisa.gov.et/)[official portal](https://www.evisa.gov.et/).


**Language in Ethiopia**

With over **80 languages**, Ethiopia is incredibly diverse. **Amharic** is widely spoken, and **English** is common in cities like Addis Ababa and Lalibela.

**Best Time to Visit Ethiopia**

Plan your trip between **November to February**, the dry season ideal for visiting **Lalibela churches** and enjoying comfortable weather across the **highlands of Ethiopia**.

**Currency in Ethiopia**

US Dollars and cards work in major cities, but always carry some **Ethiopian Birr** for rural areas and local markets.


**How Long to Spend in Lalibela**

Minimum **2 full days** in Lalibela is essential. For a broader Ethiopian adventure (Simien Mountains, Omo Valley, etc.), budget **3 weeks**.

**Internal Flights: Addis to Lalibela**

Book your **Addis Ababa to Lalibela flights** at least 2–3 weeks in advance. Window seats are highly recommended for breathtaking aerial views of the Ethiopian highlands.

**Costs & Local Guide**

I booked through a **trusted local guide** who ensured safety, cultural immersion, and smooth logistics. Highly recommend a guide during times of unrest. DM me for contact!

**Solo Travel & Safety in Ethiopia**

In **Addis Ababa**, be alert like in any big city—avoid flashing valuables. I hired a **local driver** to maximize my short stay safely.

In Lalibela, while a guide isn’t mandatory, it’s **strongly recommended** due to potential tensions. **Flights can get canceled** last-minute, so travel with flexibility and patience.

**Lalibela **

Lalibela’s rock-hewn churches in Ethiopia are the ultimate portals to another era. These architectural wonders, carved with divine precision in the 12th century, stand as living testaments to faith, craftsmanship, and a touch of magic.

** Bete Maryam**

The House of Mary rises like a stone symphony, filled with ancient frescoes and silence that speaks volumes.

My fingers traced the cool contours of ancient symbols etched into the walls, each glyph a silent prayer whispering through millennia. Here, beneath the sun's embrace, I wasn't just a tourist; I was a pilgrim, following the dusty footsteps of faith laid a thousand years ago.

**Bete Golgotha Mikael**

Descending into this cave-like church, candlelight flickers on monk robes, hymns echo off the cool rock walls—it’s a full **spiritual immersion**.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/ethiopia-s-rock-hewn-wonders-exploring-the-lalibela-churches-37.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/ethiopia-s-rock-hewn-wonders-exploring-the-lalibela-churches-39.jpg)

Monks in flowing white robes materialized from the shadows, their voices filling the cavern with ancient hymns that sent shivers down my spine. These **11 medieval churches of Lalibela**, carved inside and outside rock, defy architectural norms and tell stories of **Christian faith**, resilience, and ancient genius.
