Our latest adventure? **Camping in Dubai**—but not just any camping. Intrigued? So were we! I quickly reached out to the Campr team to plan our getaway.

**Booking Your Campr Experience**

The process is surprisingly straightforward. Here’s how it works:

**Step 1:** Visit the **Campr Dubai** [website](https://www.campr.ae/) and choose your camping package. Options range from one-night stays to four-weekend experiences. We chose the Campr2 package for one night. You’ll also select the date, number of guests, and location—desert, wadi, or mountain.

We opted for the desert to maximize our chances of star-gazing and soaking in the early “winter” chill.

**Step 2:** Wait for a response. Christine from the team replied in less than two hours, providing all the details including what’s included and payment instructions.

**Step 3:** Review costs and inclusions.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/dubaidesertcamping-3.jpg)

Desert stay in Dubai: AED 950

- Wadi or mountain stays: AED 950 + AED 300 for transportation outside Dubai
Wadi or mountain stays: AED 950 + AED 300 for transportation outside Dubai

- Optional add-ons like food, drinks, and mobile toilets are extra. If you’ve done other camping trips, you’ll know that toilets aren’t always necessary for a one-night desert escape. 
Optional add-ons like food, drinks, and mobile toilets are extra. If you’ve done other camping trips, you’ll know that toilets aren’t always necessary for a one-night desert escape. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/dubaidesertcamping-4.jpg)

A lot of people asked me about the toilet situation - there are two options you can add on for a cost; a mobile toilet unit and a split toilet/shower unit. Having used both these options in [New Zealand](/post/comprehensive-camper-van-guide-to-spending-winter-in-new-zealand), I don't find them very comfortable. There are rarely any toilets needed out in the wild. We are used to this thanks to our other camping trips, so it was no issue for us (since we just stayed the night).  

**Step 4:** You’re all set. The Campr Dubai team sends reminders of what to pack—coffee, milk, sugar, etc.—and three days before your trip, you’ll receive an approximate location. The exact spot remains a small surprise, which we loved.

**The Desert Experience**

We got our precise location a few hours before departure. Forty-five minutes later, we arrived at a stunning desert landscape. The team had set everything up, including a massive tent that easily fit the two of us. Christine gave us a quick walkthrough of the inventory and tent setup.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/dubaidesertcamping-5.jpg)

The wind was strong initially but calmed by 6 p.m., allowing us to enjoy the crisp evening air. The tent was sturdy, spacious, and cozy. Unlike most camping trips where you wake up sore, the queen mattress was plush and surprisingly comfortable. Don’t forget your pillows, bedsheets, and quilt, though!

Special mention to the massive tent set up that comes up with the package. We could have sat there for hours just taking in the fresh brisk air and endless sand dunes.

![Blog Image](/blog-images/dubaidesertcamping-8.jpg)

### **Sunset and Dinner**

Before sunset, I cozied up inside the tent while my partner wandered the dunes. The view was phenomenal, and it reminded me why **Dubai overnight camping** is such a unique experience.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/dubaidesertcamping-15.jpg)

The queen mattress was plush and extremely comfortable; I'll be honest, we usually end up with a sore back after a night out camping but not this time around. We slept like babies. 

**Don't forget to carry your own pillows, bedsheet and quilt. **
