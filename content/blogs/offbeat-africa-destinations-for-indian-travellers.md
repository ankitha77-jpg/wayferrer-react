## Why African Travel is Different for Indian Passport Holders in 2026

India maintains strong diplomatic and economic ties across Africa.
In 2026, this means:

• Simplified visa procedures  
• Better flight connectivity  
• Stronger cultural exchange programs  
• Growing Indian communities across major African cities  
Many African nations now offer visa-on-arrival or e-visa facilities specifically for Indian passport holders.

More importantly, Indian travellers are generally welcomed as trade partners, students, entrepreneurs, and cultural visitors rather than traditional tourists.

Benefits for Indian travellers include:  
• Familiarity with Indian cuisine  
• Large Indian diaspora communities  
• Shared business and trade traditions  
• Easy access to vegetarian food in many regions

### What Makes Comoros Unique Among Africa's Easiest-Access Countries?

Comoros offers one of Africa's most traveller-friendly entry policies:

• Free 30-day visa on arrival  
• No embassy visits  
• Minimal paperwork

Located in the Indian Ocean, approximately 250 km from Zanzibar, Comoros offers:

• Pristine beaches  
• Sea turtle snorkeling  
• Family-run guesthouses  
• Fresh seafood  
• Very low tourism numbers

**Budget**

₹1,500–2,500 per day  
This is one of Africa's least-discovered island destinations.

###### Safety, Health & Practical Information for Indian Travellers

**Safety**  
•	Petty theft exists in major cities  
•	Avoid displaying expensive items  
•	Use registered taxis or ride apps  
•	Follow standard travel precautions

**Health**  
Recommended:  
•	Yellow Fever vaccination  
•	Typhoid vaccination  
•	Hepatitis A vaccination  
•	Travel insurance

**Insurance Cost**  
₹1,500–3,000

**Money**  
•	Carry USD for emergencies  
•	Use ATMs in larger cities  
•	Inform your bank before travel  
•	Avoid unofficial currency exchanges

**Best Time to Visit**  
Most destinations are best visited between:  
**May – October**
This period offers:  
•	Dry weather  
•	Better wildlife viewing  
•	Comfortable temperatures
