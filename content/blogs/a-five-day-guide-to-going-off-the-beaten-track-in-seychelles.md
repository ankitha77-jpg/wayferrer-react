## **FLIGHTS + VISA**

We flew direct with Emirates. NO visa required for Indian passport holders (I believe this is the case for most countries). The immigration process was quick and simple though the line was a little long. Don't forget to have your return ticket and confirmed hotel reservation on hand! For those searching, yes, there is a **Seychelles visa for Indians** option with visa-on-arrival.

On the way back, we used the airport lounge and it was a great place to spend a few hours far away from the airport crowds.

![FLIGHTS + VISA ](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-7.jpg)

## **BEST TIME TO VISIT THINGS TO DO SEYCHELLES**

April, May, June and October, November is best due to the slightly cooler weather. This is considered the **Seychelles best time to visit** for both couples and families.

It is a French speaking country but everyone is helpful and fluent in English.

Travel Insurance is highly recommended. USD accepted at all resorts. Carry local currency for small purchases.

No trip to Seychelles is complete without island hops to most of the most popular islands - Praslin and **La Digue island**. [Cat Cocos](https://catcocos.com/) is your friend. You can go to their website, check the schedules, pay and reserve your tickets.

Our first stop in Praslin was the **_Vallee de mai _**(Valley of May). This must visit destination and a UNESCO world heritage site allows you walk through a mystical palm forest, which is also home to the iconic emblems of Seychelles - the_ coco de mer nut _and one of the rarest flora on the planet.

![flora on the planet](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-41.jpg)

The paths inside the park are very well marked and there is no way you can get lost. Some of the trees insider there are actually over 200 years old.


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-43.jpg)

No ifs, no buts - just the coco de mer nut that’s shaped like a butt. This rare giant nut is endemic to the island of Seychelles - a treasured cultural and botanical icon of the Seychelles. It is known to produce the largest and heaviest seeds in the world. It has an endangered status because this plant has separate male and female parts that can take almost 50 years to reach maturity. It is often sold for thousands of dollars - even illegally. Conservationists have turned to the islanders to help secure the palm’s future. If you happen to visit the island of Praslin, a trip to Vallee De Mai is a must, you can find out all about this nuts history straight from the locals!

![coco de mer nut ](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-45.jpg)

**HIKING THE DAN GALLAS TRAIL**

One of the best hikes in Seychelles. Incredible views, granite boulders, and Robinson-Crusoe style isolation.

![HIKING THE DAN GALLAS TRAIL](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-54.jpg)

All of the popular hiking trail can be found inside the Morne National Park, a protected National Park since 1979. You will find endemic plants, insects and birds inside the forest.. lots of sunshine, pure air and a dense forest. The perfect recipe for a memorable natural experience.


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-56.jpg)

This trail is right for you if you're looking for some adventurous trekking. As you climb up the trek, you are greeted by thick woods and granite boulders. There are steps and ladders to help you reach the summit. At the summit, you can have a spectacular birds' eye view of Victoria, Beau Vallon and the Silhouette Island on the other.

Exploring mangroves, hidden waterfalls and the stunning Grand Anse beach, followed by the beauty of Anse Royale.

![GRAND ANSE WATERFALLS](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-74.jpg)

First stop the **Port Launay Mangrove Forest **- this mangrove is one of the best and last remaining mangrove forests on the island. This area is right next to the [Constance Ephelia area](https://www.stay22.com/allez/roam?aid=wayfarerfootprints&campaign=ankitha77wixsite-afivedayguidetogoingoffthebeatentrackinseychelles&product=lma&habl=false&isinc=true&sid22=vD3M8GEzbfrFwZw0&adStack=google&hasCTZ=false&hasTP=false&conmeth=normal&plng=en&pageCategory=travel&source=direct&medium=hyperlink&address=Seychelles&hotelname=Constance+Ephelia&search=Constance+Ephelia+area&category=accommodation&version=surface-the-opportunities%7C7%7C1.2.0&ref22=https%3A%2F%2Fankitha77.wixsite.com%2Fwayfarerfootprints%2Fpost%2Fa-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles). The mangrove forests grow to a certain size and then the lodge teams plant them in the mud, in the hope that these areas would eventually be populated with more trees.

![Grand Anse waterfall](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-76.jpg)

Next stop - an extremely difficult to find **Grand Anse waterfall** in the middle of nowhere. We had to ask people about the location; we even had a local actually drive us to the spot. We finally parked our car in front of a house and took the 25 minute hike up through the lush jungle.


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-78.jpg)


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-79.jpg)


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-80.jpg)

Note that the path is marked as dangerous and rightly so - there was basically no trail. IT was just going down a pile of rocks and climbing up. If you don't have firm footing or good shoes, I DON'T RECOMMEND THIS HIKE.  It was still a fabulous getaway in the middle of a warm summer day. Worth it, if you can find your way there.

![Grand Anse beach on West Mahe](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-81.jpg)

After lunch on the way, we stopped by the gorgeous **Grand Anse beach on West Mahe**.

![Mahe western coast - beache](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-83.jpg)

We later discovered that this is one of the longest beaches on the Mahe western coast. This sprawling bay is incredibly stunning, with clean sand and a pristine turquoise ocean. The backdrop is endless mountainous landscape, perfectly contrasting the flat beach below.

![Anse Royale.](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-85.jpg)

Our last stop for the day was my favorite - behold the beauty of **Anse Royale. **


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-87.jpg)


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-88.jpg)


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-89.jpg)

I was just stunned by the changing colors, the majestic boulders, the seaweed and just everything. What could be more jaw dropping than witnessing this and having it all to yourself?

![EAST AND WEST MAHE + GRAND ANSE WATERFALLS](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-90.jpg)

The morning was spent at Baie Lazare beach, part of our **Seychelles itinerary**.

![LAST MORNING - breakfast](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-92.jpg)

The morning was spent at the Baie Lazare beach. What I loved about this beach was that it felt like a more private beach - it was quiet and used only by the people in [the hotel](https://www.stay22.com/allez/roam?aid=wayfarerfootprints&campaign=ankitha77wixsite-afivedayguidetogoingoffthebeatentrackinseychelles&product=lma&habl=false&isinc=true&sid22=vD3M8GEzbfrFwZw0&adStack=google&hasCTZ=false&hasTP=false&conmeth=normal&plng=en&pageCategory=travel&source=direct&medium=hyperlink&address=Baie+Lazare+beach%2C+Seychelles&search=the+hotel&category=accommodation&version=surface-the-opportunities%7C7%7C1.2.0&ref22=https%3A%2F%2Fankitha77.wixsite.com%2Fwayfarerfootprints%2Fpost%2Fa-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles) - though, by law, no beaches are allowed to be private in Seychelles!


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-94.jpg)


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-95.jpg)


![things to do in Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-96.jpg)

Seychelles was never a bucket list for me but turned into an unforgettable island escape. It’s expensive, but whether you’re planning a **Seychelles honeymoon**, calculating your **Seychelles trip cost**, or exploring solo, you’ll find magic

![FINAL THOUGHTS - Seychelles](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-97.jpg)

My biggest tip is for you to decide where you wish to stay and how much you want to spend. Seychelles is expense, no two ways about it. Be extra careful when you pick the resort and island you want to stay in ahead of time. While Mahe had a plenty of activities, Praslin had the best beaches. Even if Praslin was open for staying, I would not have stayed in Praslin...  but that is just me!

![Seychelles jungle forests](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-99.jpg)

Rest assured though, it is still a remarkable place to relax and unwind. From Mahe through Praslin to La Digue, you cannot go wrong with laying on empty beaches and hiking through jungle forests.

![beaches and hiking through jungle forests](/blog-images/a-five-day-guide-to-going-off-the-beaten-track-in-seychelles-101.jpg)

From Mahe through Praslin to La Digue, you cannot go wrong with relaxing on **Seychelles beaches** and enjoying the islands at your own pace.
