### **7-Day Morocco Itinerary**


### **Day 1 – Casablanca**


![Blog Image](/blog-images/morocco-itinerary-7-days-18.jpg)

**Hassan II Mosque** – Start your day with a visit to this architectural masterpiece, one of the largest mosques in the world. Built partly over the Atlantic Ocean, its intricate tilework, grand minaret, and sea-view prayer hall make it a breathtaking symbol of Moroccan craftsmanship and faith.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/morocco-itinerary-7-days-19.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/morocco-itinerary-7-days-21.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/morocco-itinerary-7-days-23.jpg)


### **United Nations Square** – The cultural crossroads of Casablanca, this bustling plaza marks the transition between the French colonial Ville Nouvelle and the historic Old Medina. It’s a great spot to observe everyday life and catch a glimpse of the city’s layered identity.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/morocco-itinerary-7-days-25.jpg)

**Casablanca Medina** – While smaller than those in Fez or Marrakech, the medina here offers an authentic peek into the city’s past. Wander its narrow lanes to discover traditional vendors, crumbling colonial facades, and remnants of **Anfa**, the ancient Roman port that gave Casablanca its roots.

**Ville Nouvelle** – Designed during the French Protectorate, this district is Casablanca’s modern heartbeat. Tree-lined boulevards, elegant art deco buildings, stylish boutiques, and European-style cafés paint a portrait of the city’s cosmopolitan side. Don’t miss the chance to sip mint tea while watching the city’s youthful, creative energy unfold.

**IMPORTANT PLANNING TIPS**
Logistics matter when you're covering four cities in seven days. The beauty of Morocco's geography - with Casablanca, Chefchaouen, Fez, and Meknes positioned within a few hours of each other - is that independent travel becomes not just possible but genuinely affordable. Moving between these cities means handling multiple transitions, which is why having a reliable rental car or booking transfers in advance becomes crucial. With your own wheels, you avoid the rigid schedules of organized tours and gain the freedom to chase sunrise at Akchour Falls or linger in a hidden Fez alley without watching the clock. 

Before your trip, [compare your car rental options](https://www.discovercars.com/morocco) to lock in competitive rates and secure a well-maintained vehicle, especially important since Morocco's inter-city roads vary in condition and you'll want peace of mind on mountain passes. Renting early also lets you choose what works for your travel style: a nimble hatchback for medina navigation, or something sturdier if you're considering day trips into the Atlas region. The time you save planning these logistics upfront translates directly into more time experiencing what made this itinerary unforgettable.
