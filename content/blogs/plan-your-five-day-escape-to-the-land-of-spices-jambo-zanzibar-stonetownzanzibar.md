### **Flights ****+**** Visa**** ****Stone Town Zanzibar**

I flew direct with [Fly Dubai](https://www.expedia.com/). The flight was 5 hours and 45 minutes.

Visa is on arrival for most nationalities, including Indian citizens. **Zanzibar visa for Indians** costs $50 per person. No other documents are needed. Best to carry cash. You can also submit your visa application online before you travel.


The **best time to visit Zanzibar** is June to October due to slightly cooler weather. December to February is also dry but the afternoon heat can hit hard.

Everyone is helpful and fairly fluent in English. USD is accepted at most resorts.

USD accepted at all resorts. 

I prefer hiring a local guide/driver in [Africa.](/africa) This supports the local economy, is safer, and gives access to hidden gems and history.

After much deliberation, I hired a local taxi. The car was a comfortable SUV in great condition, stocked with water. He was professional, neat, punctual, and knew all the best spots. Costs vary depending on your activities.

The car was a comfortable SUV in great condition, stocked with water, which you will need a lot of.** **Felt completely safe with him - he was perfectly dressed, neat, professional and extremely punctual. The cost depends on what you choose to do during your trip. 

I traveled with a friend, but also explored solo. Luxury properties made me feel safe. There was some minor catcalling in Stone Town, but walking with purpose and following your instincts ensures safety. Avoid wandering alone at night.

### **Day 1 – Jozani Forest Zanzibar & Stone Town**

After breakfast, a one-hour drive took us to **Jozani Forest Zanzibar**, home to red colobus monkeys. Guides help you spot the monkeys and bushbabies safely.

Fun fact: Colobus monkeys have only four fingers and use their hands, not tails, to move between trees.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-11.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-13.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-15.jpg)

This tour is not a safari so please don't mistake it for one. That being said, the monkeys are sneaky.. there will be some of coordination between the guides. It doesn't take very long to spot the little guys up on trees. They are wild animals and it is recommended you stand atleast 5ft away from them **(please be mindful if you a flu or cold as you could pass this on to them!).** They carry on with their business in a relaxed manner as if you weren't around.  If you are lucky, you can also spot some bushbaby. 

Afterward, return to Park Hyatt Zanzibar to explore the winding streets of **Stone Town Zanzibar**. The hotel merges contemporary design with old-world charm, offering stunning views.

**Pro Planning Tip: if you have an extra day or time to kill, think about including this forest on your way to the Paje beach. **

The guide was wonderful. He had so much information about the place and monkeys. For instance, what makes the colobus monkeys unique is that they only have four fingers. Also, unlike other species of monkeys, they don't jump from tree to tree using their tails. Their tails don't have muscles which means they use their hands. Cool huh? 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-23.jpg)

### **Day 4 – Swimming with Horses**

Early morning pool and beach exploration. Miles of soft white sand leading into turquoise waters.

Afternoon: Swim with horses on Nungwi beach (60-minute experience, $50). The rescued horses are treated compassionately, ensuring a safe and ethical experience.

Sunset: Relaxed evening on the beach and resort grounds.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-171.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-173.jpg)

I did my research to make sure the adopted horses are treated compassionately and the interactions are sensitive. I chose not to climb on Bob until we got to the beach. I walked alongside, hoping he would trust me. When he took his time and chose to roll around in the sand instead, I remember being very nervous. What had I done to make him cautious? This is something I wanted to make certain, no animal should be forced to engage in an activity. After 10 minutes, he was excited about the waves but I was still weary about climbing on; tbh I was overwhelmed by this (powerful) little guy. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-175.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-177.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-179.jpg)

I held on lightly so as to not cause his back any harm (fell off a fair few times in the process). I was on edge, making sure he was comfortable in the high tides, I kept speaking softly. My confidence was bolstered when he didn’t waver for a minute, swimming bravely with me by his side. At the end of it, he had endeared to me.. we even did a little head bump. Moments forever etched in my heart. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-181.jpg)

I cannot tell you what a meaningful experience this was, free of saddles, bonding with this magnificent animal. It was about being respectful and treating him as an equal. I simply let Bob lead the way. I only followed - not the other way around! 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/plan-your-five-day-escape-to-the-land-of-spices-jambo-zanzibar-stonetownzanzibar-182.jpg)
