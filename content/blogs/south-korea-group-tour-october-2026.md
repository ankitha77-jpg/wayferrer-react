## Why October is South Korea's Best Travel Season in 2026

October offers:

• Perfect weather (17–23°C) with minimal rainfall  
• Brilliant autumn colors across temples and mountains  
• Lower prices than summer peak season  
• Fewer tourists compared to July and August

Summer is scorching and humid. Winter brings snow and ski crowds. October is genuinely the sweet spot—visually stunning, comfortable, affordable, and uncrowded. South Korea's trees at peak color are among Asia's most spectacular natural sights.

### What Exactly Happens During the Hallasan Mountain Hike in October?
Hallasan is South Korea's highest peak at 1,950 metres and requires approximately 5–6 hours for a round-trip hike.
In October, the mountain transforms into a spectacular display of autumn colours. As you ascend, the landscape changes from green to gold and rust tones, with the forest colours becoming more dramatic at higher elevations.

Highlights include:  
•	Endemic plant species  
•	Late-season wildflowers  
•	Panoramic views across Jeju Island  
•	Clear-day views towards mainland Korea  

For many travellers, this is the most memorable experience of the entire tour.

### Which Seoul Palaces Offer the Best Autumn Colours in October?
 
**Gyeongbokgung Palace**  
The largest and most iconic palace in Seoul, offering beautiful autumn scenery throughout its grounds.  

**Changdeokgung Palace**  
Home to the famous Secret Garden, widely regarded as one of Seoul's most beautiful autumn locations.  

**Deoksugung Palace**  
A unique blend of traditional architecture and modern city surroundings.  

**Jogyesa Temple & Other Monasteries**  
Perfect for experiencing autumn colours in a peaceful spiritual setting.
October provides exceptional photography opportunities, with vibrant colours throughout Seoul's historic sites.

The hike is considered moderate in difficulty.
