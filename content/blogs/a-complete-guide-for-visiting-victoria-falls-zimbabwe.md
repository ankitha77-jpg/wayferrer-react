**Part four and the final stretch of our amazing Southern Africa trip. Let’s call this adrenaline and indulgence.**

This country was meant to be combined with [Zambia](/post/zambia) last December, but we were forced to push it. I was determined to make it back as soon as possible to see the **Zimbabwe** side of **Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe**.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-1.jpg)

There is no shortage of spectacular waterfalls around the globe, but seeing the mighty Victoria Falls is a unique experience because it feels raw. The way you can experience it is outstanding and more memorable than many other falls. With the borders reopening, it’s as good a time as any to visit this waterfall, which is actually twice as high as Niagara Falls and 500m wider!


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-2.jpg)

Here is my **Victoria Falls itinerary** and guide for you to plan a trip to this **UNESCO World Heritage Site** and one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.

- Cross over from Botswana or Zambia or fly directly to Zimbabwe

- Clear border immigration, check into **Ilala Lodge Victoria Falls**, visiting the Mosi-O-Tunya park

- **Zipline Victoria Falls** experience

- Check in and explore **Palm River Hotel Zimbabwe**

- Breakfast, R&R, fly home in the evening

If flying directly to Zimbabwe from Dubai, you can fly with [Emirates](https://www.expedia.com/Flights).

If connecting via other countries, I recommend what we did — fly from Johannesburg to **Victoria Falls Zimbabwe** (land and get a multiple-entry visa for $50).

At the road border for Indian passport holders:

- Visa on arrival is available ($50 for multiple entry, which works well if visiting Botswana)

- Immigration can take time, so plan accordingly

Double-vaccination proof is mandatory

This isn’t as safe as Botswana, so exert caution and go with a trustworthy **Wild Horizons Victoria Falls** guide. Also, be mindful of extra costs when going solo in this part of the world.. 

Depending on your plans, timing is key. The ideal time to see the falls is April–June.

If you want to do **Devil’s Pool**, you can only go between late September–early December when water levels are safe.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-3.jpg)

**Language and People**

English is widely spoken here, as in many parts of Africa, making travel smooth and communication easy.

Travel insurance is highly recommended — try World Nomads. Carry local currency for small purchases and tipping.

You don’t need a guide for just Victoria Falls; your lodge can arrange all activities, from **sunset cruise Victoria Falls** bookings to airport transfers. If planning a safari, hire a guide.

Not as pricey as Botswana or Zambia, but high-end **luxury hotels in Zimbabwe** will still set you back around $2,000 for 3–4 nights. The Zimbabwe side is generally cheaper than the Zambia side for **Victoria Falls luxury hotels**.

We stayed at **Ilala Lodge Victoria Falls** and later the five-star boutique **Palm River Hotel Zimbabwe** — both fantastic choices if you’re wondering **where to stay in Victoria Falls**.

No issues at our lodges, and I highly recommend **Three Monkeys Victoria Falls** bar opposite Ilala Lodge — great vegan options, amazing vibe. Book in advance.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-4.jpg)

After checking into Ilala Lodge, we walked 15 minutes to the **Victoria Falls Rainforest**. Entry is $30 per person. Arrive by 3 p.m. to enjoy till closing at 6 p.m. The forest walk is easy and family-friendly..


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-5.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-7.jpg)

As you follow the marked points, the power of the falls creates a white haze that obscures the view until you reach the 13th point — the “Burning Point,” where Zimbabwe meets Zambia.

**T****Travel Tip:** Bring a poncho and towel — you will get wet.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-9.jpg)

[Zipline](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g293761-d2482830-Reviews-Wild_Horizons-Victoria_Falls_Matabeleland_North_Province.html)** Victoria Falls**

Since we already did Devil’s Pool, we opted for the famous **zipline Victoria Falls** with **Wild Horizons Victoria Falls**. At $120 for a tandem ride, it’s one of Africa’s longest ziplines, reaching speeds over 100 kph. You’ll be suspended 120 meters above the **Zambezi River hotels** area, with incredible views.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-10.jpg)

Other adrenaline-packed **Victoria Falls activities** include the **helicopter ride Victoria Falls**, bungee jumping, white-water rafting, and the flying fox.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-11.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-13.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-15.jpg)

This is a fantastic opportunity to see both the sides of the waterfall as you are suspended 120 meters above the Zambezi river. If you are an adrenaline junkie, you don't want to miss one. There are other great activities to do here like helicopter ride over the falls, bungee jumping, white water rafting or flying fox. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-17.jpg)

Our last two days were pure indulgence — riverside dining, **Victoria Falls high tea**, cocktails, and sunsets over the Zambezi. [The Palm River Hotel](https://www.booking.com/hotel/zw/palm-river.en-gb.html?chal_t=1757511816028&force_referer=) is one of the **best luxury hotels in Zimbabwe** for a peaceful finale to an adventure-filled trip. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-18.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/staying-at-the-palm-river-hotel-zimbabwe-victoriafallshotel-24.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-22.jpg)

From dawn to dusk, we had the most amazing food, drinks, high-tea and sunset views. We could not have asked for a better ending to a fantastic two weeks in our favorite continent. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/staying-at-the-palm-river-hotel-zimbabwe-victoriafallshotel-26.jpg)

The Zimbabwe side truly delivered — dramatic views, thrilling activities, and world-class **Victoria Falls accommodation**. For the ultimate experience, combine it with Zambia: enjoy Devil’s Pool from the Zambia side, and breathtaking views from Zimbabwe.


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-cover.jpg)

The best way to visit Victoria Falls is to combine it with a trip to Zambia. You can only do the Devils pool from Zambia but the views are a hundred times better on the Zimbabwe side. If you are short on time, choose to stay on one side and cross over to the other side. We loved both the resorts, the high-end resorts offer just the right indulgence after adrenaline packed adventures. 


![Blog Image](/blog-images/a-complete-guide-for-visiting-victoria-falls-zimbabwe-26.jpg)


![Blog Image](/blog-images/staying-at-the-palm-river-hotel-zimbabwe-victoriafallshotel-cover.jpg)

More than a month later, we’re still reminiscing about soaring over one of the world’s greatest waterfalls, the Zambezi flowing between two nations, and evenings spent in sheer comfort at some of the **best hotels in Victoria Falls**.
